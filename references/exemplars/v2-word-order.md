# Exemplars: V2 Word Order & Dutch Sentence Structure

Gold-standard examples of correct V2 inversion in Dutch subtitles.
Sources: The Remains of the Day (1993), The World At War (1973).

---

## The Rule

Dutch requires the finite verb in SECOND position. When any element other than
the subject starts the sentence, the subject and verb INVERT.

English: "Then he left." → Dutch: "Toen vertrok hij." (NOT "Toen hij vertrok.")
The fronted element (Toen) takes position 1. The verb (vertrok) takes position 2.
The subject (hij) follows the verb.

---

## Time Adverbs Triggering Inversion

### "Enkele uren later"
EN: "Only a few hours later Holland decided to surrender."
NL: "Enkele uren later gaf 't land zich over."
WHY: "Enkele uren later" fronted → verb "gaf" in position 2 → subject "'t land" follows. WRONG would be: "Enkele uren later 't land gaf zich over."

### "Vroeger"
EN: "The world always used to come to this house."
NL: "Vroeger kwam de wereld hierheen."
WHY: "Vroeger" fronted → "kwam" in P2 → "de wereld" follows. Natural Dutch sentence rhythm.

### "Binnen een kwartier"
EN: "In 15 minutes they started fires..."
NL: "Binnen een kwartier stond 't centrum in lichterlaaie..."
WHY: Prepositional phrase fronted → "stond" in P2. This creates dramatic impact — the timeframe hits first.

### "Voor het laatst"
EN: "It was the last time His Lordship was happy to welcome his neighbors."
NL: "Voor het laatst was de graaf blij om z'n buren te ontvangen."
WHY: "Voor het laatst" fronted → "was" in P2 → "de graaf" follows.

## Conjunctive Adverbs Triggering Inversion

### "Daarom"
EN: "That's exactly what makes it so abominable."
NL: "Daarom is het net zo verachtelijk."
WHY: "Daarom" → "is" in P2 → "het" follows.

### "Misschien"
EN: "The occupation might not be so bad after all."
NL: "Misschien viel die bezetting wel mee."
WHY: "Misschien" → "viel" in P2 → "die bezetting" follows.

## After Subordinate Clauses

### "Terwijl... [main clause]"
EN: "That night, as Rotterdam blazed, the Dutch people were leaderless."
NL: "Terwijl Rotterdam brandde, zat 't volk zonder regering."
WHY: Subordinate clause first ("Terwijl Rotterdam brandde") → main clause inverted: "zat" (V2) → "'t volk" (subject). The subordinate clause as a whole occupies position 1.

### "Zolang... [main clause]"
EN: "So long as we didn't bother them, they accept everything."
NL: "Zolang wij hen niet dwarszaten, konden we onze gang gaan."
WHY: Subordinate "Zolang..." → main clause inverted: "konden" in P2 → "we" follows. Note: within the subordinate clause itself, the verb goes to the END ("dwarszaten" at the end).

### "Als... [main clause]"
EN: "Once the staff start marrying, the house is at sixes and sevens."
NL: "...een huis ontregeld raakt als personeel trouwt."
WHY: Here the subordinate clause follows — no inversion in the main clause needed. Inversion only triggers when the subordinate clause is FRONTED.

## Object Fronting for Emphasis

### Emphasized object
EN: "This I consider my private place of work."
NL: "Dit beschouw ik als m'n werkruimte."
WHY: "Dit" (object) fronted for emphasis → "beschouw" in P2 → "ik" follows.

## Common V2 Mistakes to Avoid

### WRONG: Subject-verb order after fronted adverb
"Vroeger de wereld kwam hierheen." ← WRONG
"Vroeger kwam de wereld hierheen." ← CORRECT

### WRONG: V2 inside subordinate clause
"...omdat hij kwam vroeg thuis." ← WRONG (verb should be at END in subordinate)
"...omdat hij vroeg thuiskwam." ← CORRECT

### WRONG: No inversion after coordinating conjunction
"En toen vertrok hij." ← might seem correct but "en" is coordinating, "toen" is the adverb
"En toen vertrok hij." ← actually CORRECT here — "toen" triggers inversion
"En hij vertrok toen." ← also correct — different emphasis

### Continuation after ellipsis: inversion still applies
EN: "Still... we are at war."
NL: "Nog steeds... zijn wij in oorlog."
WHY: The ellipsis doesn't reset V2 — "Nog steeds" is still position 1. "Zijn" must be position 2.
